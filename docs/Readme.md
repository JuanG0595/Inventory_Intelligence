## Case Study

The main objective of an inventory optimization project using Excel and Power Query is to balance product availability to meet customer demand while minimizing operating and storage costs.

In this case, our problem is as follows: "The company detected a drop in profitability (symptom). The hypothesis is that capital is tied up in slow-moving products (actual problem)."

*Key Question:* "How can we reduce stock levels by 20% without affecting sales?"

## Key Steps to Implement:

### 1. Operational Efficiency and Automation

*Enterprise Process Automation (ETL):* Use Power Query to automatically extract, transform, and load data from multiple sources (CSVs, databases, or cloud services), eliminating the need for manual copying and pasting.

*Large Volume Management:* Process extensive datasets with higher performance than traditional Excel formulas, improving file stability.

### 2. Strategic Stock Control

*Stock Balancing:* Avoid stockouts that impact sales and overstocking that unnecessarily ties up capital.

*Real-Time Visibility:* Centralize information to know when to order, how much to order, and where to store, facilitating data-driven decision-making.

### 3. Cost and Loss Reduction

*Minimizing Expenses:* Reduce the total cost of warehousing and inventory management operations.

*Loss Prevention:* Identify slow-moving products to prevent waste or financial losses due to obsolescence.

### 4. Scalability and Advanced Analysis

*Data Modeling:* Facilitate the creation of custom fields and data normalization for more accurate statistical and trend analysis.

*Low-Cost Solution:* Implement a professional control tool without the need to purchase expensive specialized software licenses.

## Challenges Encountered:

Since the original records were purely transactional, a master inventory table was designed to allow the calculation of turnover metrics and critical replenishment levels, transforming a sales report into a supply management tool.

Statistical profiling was performed on approximately 215,000 transactional records to derive a balanced inventory structure. The analysis allowed us to establish average unit costs and initial stock levels based on historical demand for the four key quarters of 2019.

## Key Metrics to Consider:

*Inventory Turnover:* How many times do we sell the total stock of a product in a year? (Total Sales / Average Stock).

*Sell-Through Rate:* What percentage of the received inventory was actually sold?

*Days of Inventory on Hand (DOH):* How many days will our current stock last if we continue selling at the same rate as in 2019?
